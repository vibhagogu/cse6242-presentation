1. Motivation

Road traffic accidents kill 1.35 million people annually and are the leading cause of death for ages 5–29 globally. Existing road safety processes remain reactive—analyzing accidents only after they occur.

Why should we care?

Transportation planners and safety agencies lack tools to explore what would happen if conditions changed. Our project fills this gap with an interactive simulator that lets stakeholders proactively identify high-risk areas and test interventions before deploying resources.

4. Our Approach — Algorithm & Interactive Visualization

What are our approaches?

  • H3 Hexagonal Spatial Binning: We partition the US into hexagonal cells at resolution 3. Hexagons provide uniform adjacency, eliminating directional bias found in square grids.
  • LightGBM Severity Prediction: A gradient-boosted decision tree model trained on the full dataset to predict accident severity (1–4).
  • Empirical Severity Multipliers: Per-feature ratios of mean severity power the What-If simulator for real-time scenario exploration.

What is new in our approach?

  • What-If Simulator: Users toggle conditions to see how severity shifts across all hexes simultaneously—transforming the tool from descriptive to prescriptive.
  • Delta View: A diverging green–grey–red color scale shows the change in severity from baseline.
  • Nationwide scale: Covers all 49 states with 7.7M records using chunked processing.

Why will it work?

  • Multipliers computed from 7.7M real observations, grounding simulations in actual data.
  • H3 binning reduces visual clutter while preserving geographic patterns.
  • LightGBM captures non-linear interactions that logit models cannot, with interpretable feature importances.

7. Comparison

vs. Statistical Models

Logit models assume linearity. Our LightGBM captures non-linear interactions and spatial dependence via H3.

vs. Neural Networks

ANNs sacrifice interpretability. LightGBM provides built-in feature importances.

vs. Existing Dashboards

Traditional dashboards are descriptive and single-region. Ours is prescriptive and covers all 49 states.

vs. Castellani et al.

They use SHAP on Ohio data. We use native importances + a real-time interactive simulator.

8. Conclusions & Future Work

Key Takeaways

  • End-to-end pipeline from 7.7M raw records to interactive geospatial dashboard with real-time What-If simulation.
  • H3 binning + empirical multipliers enable instant scenario exploration.
  • Delta View uniquely visualizes intervention impact.
  • Infrastructure features measurably reduce severity, supporting evidence-based investment.

Limitations & Future Work

  • Severity 2 dominance (~80%) limits minority-class accuracy.
  • Empirical multipliers vs. causal models.
  • Future: real-time feeds, user studies, county-level policy.
